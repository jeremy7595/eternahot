Why Upgrade

Tank vs. tankless, honestly.

Not sure whether tankless is right for your home? Here is the plain comparison — and if a tank truly suits your household better, that is what we’ll tell you.

Traditional Tank

  • Runs out of hot water
  • Heats water around the clock, even unused
  • 8–12 year lifespan
  • Takes up significant floor space
  • Higher monthly energy use

Tankless

  • Endless hot water, on demand
  • Heats only the water you use
  • 20+ year lifespan
  • Wall-mounted — reclaims floor space
  • Up to 40% less energy use

Common Questions

Everything you want to know.

How long does a residential install take?

Most residential installations are completed in 4–6 hours. If significant gas-line work or venting modifications are needed, it may run a full day. We’ll give you a realistic timeline at the time of assessment.

Do I need a permit?

Yes — Los Angeles requires a permit for water heater replacements and new installations. We handle the permit application as part of every installation. Don’t let any contractor skip this; it protects you when you sell your home.

Will a tankless unit lower my gas use?

Most homeowners see a 25–40% reduction in the energy used for water heating, because a tankless unit heats only the water you actually use instead of keeping a full tank hot around the clock.

How often should my unit be serviced?

Annually in Southern California — our hard water builds mineral scale inside the heat exchanger quickly. An annual descale takes one to two hours and extends your unit’s life by years. We’ll send you a reminder when yours is due.

What happens after the installation?

We come back. Shortly after the install — once you’ve lived with the system — we return to fine-tune it and answer questions. A year later, we return again to service and inspect it ourselves. Every installation carries our one-year workmanship guarantee.
