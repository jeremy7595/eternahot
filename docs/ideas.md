## "What NOT to do" gallery — proposed page: `/what-not-to-do`

A gallery of real photos showing piping and configurations that were installed
wrong, each paired with the consequence (scale, leaks, short-cycling, a failed
unit, water damage). The goal is **recognition**: a visitor sees something that
looks like their own home or business and calls.

**Why it works**
- Turns a passive visitor into a worried one — "that looks like mine."
- Fear of an expensive failure motivates a call more than a sales pitch does.

**Guidelines for the photos**
- Use our own real field photos — authenticity is the whole point. No stock.
- Show the consequence, not just the mistake. Each entry works best as a pair:
  the bad config → what it caused.
- Never name or identify another company or person. Critique the *work* only
  ("installed by others"), not the installer. This matters in LA.
- End every entry with the same quiet CTA: **"Recognize this? Call us."** —
  one number, no friction.

**Rough format**
- A simple photo grid. Each item: photo + one line "the problem" + one line
  "the risk." Linked from the homepage once the service-plan side is settled.

**Where the photos live**
- Collect in the field on **Google Drive → Eternahot** (easy from the Chromebook
  while out of town). Suggested subfolder: `Eternahot/what-not-to-do`.
- When there's a batch, the finalists get copied into `assets/what-not-to-do/`
  in this repo to build the page.
